America in Crisis

More Americans than ever dying from drug overdoses

2017 CDC report details staggering scale of the opioid epidemic

By ELENA COX

Published September 15, 2019

This story originally came from The Guardian. I edited and recreated graphics and text for this piece for a course in the Columbia Journalism School.

Data released in 2017 by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention found more Americans than ever were dying from drug overdoses. The problem began in the 1990s, when U.S. pharmaceutical companies started aggressively lobbying doctors to prescribe drugs like OxyContin, which they claimed could provide effective pain relief with no real risk of addiction. Since then, prescription opioid use in the U.S. has more than tripled.
